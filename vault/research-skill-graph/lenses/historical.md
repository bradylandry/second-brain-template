# Lens: Historical

What patterns repeat? What's been tried before? What context is everyone forgetting?

## Core Questions
1. When has this exact problem been solved before, and how?
2. What has been tried and failed? Why did it fail?
3. What cycles or patterns are visible in the data over time?
4. What context do current observers typically ignore?
5. What did the landscape look like 10 years ago vs. today?

## ACT-Specific Questions
- How has ACT test-taking trended since the test-optional movement started (~2020)?
- What happened to SAT/ACT prep companies after COVID? Who survived, who didn't?
- How did Khan Academy's free SAT prep affect the market when it launched (2015)?
- What ed-tech companies raised a lot of money, failed, and why? (Knewton, Pearson digital, etc.)
- How has the TOPS scholarship threshold changed over time? Does score inflation affect it?
- What happened to Magoosh, PrepScholar, Varsity Tutors — growth trajectory?
- When has "AI tutoring" been tried before? What worked, what didn't?

## How to Research Through This Lens
- Look for cycles, not just trends
- Find the failed experiments — they contain more signal than successes
- Trace the origin of current market structures: why does in-person tutoring cost $100-200/hr? When did that price emerge?
- Check Crunchbase, TechCrunch archives for ed-tech funding/failure history

## Output Format
For each finding:
- **PERIOD:** [timeframe]
- **WHAT HAPPENED:** [event or pattern]
- **OUTCOME:** [what resulted]
- **RELEVANCE:** [why this matters for our decisions]

## Voice
Neutral historian. "In 2015, Khan Academy launched free SAT prep. Kaplan's revenue declined 12% the following year" not "Khan Academy destroyed the prep market."
