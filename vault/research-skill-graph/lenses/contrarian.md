# Lens: Contrarian

What if the consensus is wrong? Who benefits from the current narrative? What is nobody saying?

## Core Questions
1. What is the mainstream assumption about this topic?
2. What is the strongest argument that the mainstream is wrong?
3. Who benefits from the current consensus? Do they have incentive to maintain it?
4. What evidence exists that directly contradicts the consensus?
5. What would a smart, successful person who disagrees with you say?

## ACT-Specific Contrarian Takes to Stress-Test

**Consensus:** "ACT prep apps are a growing market with strong demand"
**Contrarian:** Khan Academy is free and good enough. Students who would pay for an app are already using free resources. The addressable paying market is smaller than it looks.

**Consensus:** "TOPS scholarship creates strong motivation to improve ACT scores"
**Contrarian:** Most students who will hit 20 do it naturally. The students who can't hit 20 with prep probably can't hit it without. Prep has lower ROI for the bottom quartile than assumed.

**Consensus:** "AI tutoring is the future of test prep"
**Contrarian:** The ACT tests specific, well-documented skills. A static question bank + spaced repetition beats AI tutoring for most students. AI adds cost and complexity without proportional improvement.

**Consensus:** "Mobile-first is the right approach for teen users"
**Contrarian:** Serious test prep requires focused, distraction-free study. The students most likely to improve their scores are the ones least likely to study on their phones.

**Consensus:** "The test-optional trend reduces ACT demand"
**Contrarian:** Louisiana's mandatory ACT law insulates this market. Louisiana students will take the ACT regardless. TOPS makes score improvement high-stakes regardless of what Harvard does.

## How to Research Through This Lens
- Find people who tried this and failed — what did they learn?
- Look for data that contradicts the story you've been building
- Find the smartest critic of your assumptions and steelman their argument
- Check app store reviews of competitors for what users actually hate

## Output Format
For each contrarian finding:
- **CONSENSUS:** [the mainstream view]
- **CONTRARIAN CLAIM:** [the challenge]
- **EVIDENCE FOR:** [what supports the contrarian view]
- **CONFIDENCE:** [how seriously should we take this?]
- **RESPONSE:** [how does our strategy account for this?]

## Voice
Devil's advocate. Skeptical. "Users give Magoosh 4.7 stars but churn after 30 days — they like it but don't use it" not "Magoosh is great."
