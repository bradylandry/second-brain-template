# Lens: Geopolitical

For this project, "geopolitical" means: what external forces, policies, and power structures shape the landscape? Zoom out from the product to the system it exists in.

## Core Questions
1. What policy decisions shape this market?
2. Which institutions hold power, and what are their incentives?
3. What regulatory or structural forces could change the landscape?
4. What regional dynamics matter (Louisiana vs. national)?
5. Who are the gatekeepers, and what do they want?

## ACT-Specific Questions
- What is the College Board / ACT Inc. relationship? Are they competitors or complementary?
- How do Louisiana state policies affect ACT participation? (Louisiana mandates ACT for all public school juniors — why? who funds it?)
- What is the test-optional movement's real trajectory? Are elite schools actually dropping the ACT?
- How do school districts make decisions about prep resources? Who influences them?
- What federal education funding streams touch test prep? (Title I, etc.)
- How does the Louisiana Board of Regents set TOPS thresholds? Have they ever changed them?
- What is the relationship between ACT Inc. and schools? Do they sell prep materials directly?
- What happens to demand if Louisiana changes the mandatory ACT policy?

## How to Research Through This Lens
- Look for: legislation, regulatory filings, institutional budgets, policy papers
- Identify the decision-makers in the ecosystem (state DOE, college admissions offices, ACT Inc.)
- Find where lobbying money flows in education testing

## Output Format
For each finding:
- **ACTOR:** [institution, policy, or force]
- **POWER:** [what they control]
- **INCENTIVE:** [what they want]
- **RISK/OPPORTUNITY:** [what this means for the ACT app]

## Voice
Structural and unsentimental. "Louisiana Board of Regents sets TOPS thresholds. They have not raised the minimum score since 2015, despite score inflation" not "the system is unfair."
