# Open Questions — ACT Prep Market

These are unresolved questions after the research. They are as valuable as what we found — they define what to research next and what assumptions to validate before making big decisions.

---

## High Priority (decisions depend on these)

**1. What is the actual TOPS qualifying rate by income bracket?**
If TOPS recipients skew middle/upper-middle income, willingness to pay $49 is high. If they skew low-income, we may need a free tier or scholarship model. LOSFA publishes this data — retrieve it.
*Where to find: losfa.la.gov, TOPS annual reports*

**2. What are the app's current retention metrics?**
Day-7 and Day-30 retention rates will tell us if FSRS is actually creating habits. All monetization math depends on this. If Day-7 retention is <20%, the funnel is broken before monetization begins.
*Where to find: Firebase Analytics in the app*

**3. Does our question bank actually cover the right content?**
See question content audit section below. This is the most urgent product question.

**4. What is the Louisiana school counselor ecosystem?**
How many counselors per school? What tools do they recommend today? What would it take to get a counselor to recommend this app to every junior? This is the B2B2C distribution unlock.
*Where to find: LDOE school directory, counselor associations*

---

## Medium Priority (important but not blocking)

**5. What do ACT format changes mean for our question bank?**
ACT has been evolving (digital ACT pilot, format adjustments). Are our questions aligned to the current test format? Do we need to audit and update?
*Where to find: ACT.org test format documentation*

**6. What high-mandate ACT states should we expand to first?**
Michigan, Wisconsin, Colorado, Alabama, Nevada, and Hawaii all have statewide ACT programs. Which has the most analogous scholarship structure to TOPS? This defines the national expansion roadmap.
*Where to find: State DOE websites, ACT state participation reports*

**7. What is the conversion rate for a score guarantee offer?**
Magoosh's 4-point guarantee is their #1 conversion driver per their marketing. What would our guarantee cost us in refunds at current score improvement rates?
*Where to find: Model based on ACT research avg gains + our user data*

---

## Low Priority (interesting but not urgent)

**8. Is there a parent-facing product opportunity?**
Parents are often the ones paying. A parent dashboard showing their child's progress toward a TOPS threshold could be a premium feature. But it adds complexity — validate demand before building.

**9. What is the Title I / ESSA funding pathway timeline?**
Getting certified as an ESSA-compliant tool opens institutional purchasing. Likely a 12–24 month process. Worth understanding the requirements now even if we don't pursue immediately.

**10. Could LOSFA or LDOE become a distribution partner?**
If the state wanted every junior using a free evidence-based prep tool, they might fund it. Long shot but worth one conversation.

---

## The Most Important Open Question
**Can we prove score improvement with our own users?**
Everything — monetization, marketing, B2B, guarantees — depends on being able to say "students who use this app improve X points on average." We need to collect before/after ACT scores from users. Build this data collection into the app now, before we have too many users to retrofit it.
