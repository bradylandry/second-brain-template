# 11th Grader ACT Testing Timeline

The optimal strategy for a Louisiana junior targeting TOPS.

---

## The Strategy in One Sentence
Use the mandatory state ACT as your free diagnostic, then prep specifically for the retake with a targeted study plan focused on your 2–3 weakest sub-skills.

---

## Full Timeline

### Summer Before Junior Year (June–August)
**Goal:** Get ahead. Most students don't start until after they see their first score — starting here gives an extra 3 months.
- Take a free full-length diagnostic (in the app or printable)
- Identify top 3 weakest sub-skills
- Begin FSRS daily review sessions: 10–15 min/day
- Study English grammar rules and core Math concepts first (highest ROI)

### Early Junior Year (September–October)
**Goal:** Baseline score + activate study plan
- Take the state-mandated ACT (free, mandatory for all Louisiana public school juniors)
- This is your official baseline — enter the score into the app
- The app calculates your gap to each TOPS threshold and activates a targeted study plan
- Don't panic if the score is lower than expected — this is exactly what the plan is for

### Active Prep Window (November–February)
**Goal:** 20–30 focused hours of targeted prep
- Daily FSRS sessions: 10–15 min (non-negotiable — this is where retention is built)
- Weekly timed section practice: 30–45 min (English or Math, alternating)
- One full-length timed practice test in December (paper or desktop — not mobile)
- Review every wrong answer with the explanation before moving on
- Track progress in the app — watch your estimated composite move toward the TOPS threshold

### First Retake — February or April (Junior Year) ⭐ Most Important
**Goal:** +3–5 composite points
- This is the highest-leverage test of your entire prep journey
- You have had months of structured prep
- You're still in school rhythm (summer brain hasn't set in)
- You have multiple retakes still available if needed
- February test date is ideal; April is the backup
- **Most students who will improve their score do so by this test**

### Second Retake — June or September (if needed)
**Goal:** Cross remaining gap if still below threshold
- June gives you the summer to prep
- September is the last comfortable shot before college apps begin
- Diminishing returns: if 3+ retakes haven't moved the score, intensive tutoring or a different approach is needed — not more of the same

### Hard Deadlines
- **October, Senior Year:** Last ACT test date with comfortable margin for score delivery
- **December 1, Senior Year:** TOPS application deadline — ACT scores must be submitted
- **Do not wait until senior year to start** — that leaves only one or two retakes with no buffer

## Section-Specific Tips

### English (highest improvement potential)
Focus on: comma rules, apostrophes, run-on sentences, sentence structure, redundancy
These are finite, learnable rules. 10 hours on English grammar rules = 2–3 English subscore points for most students.

### Math (high improvement potential)
Focus on: linear equations, systems of equations, basic geometry (area, angles), percentages
Calculator vs. no-calculator section strategy matters. Know which question types appear in each.

### Reading (medium improvement potential)
Focus on: passage mapping strategy, question types (main idea vs. detail vs. inference), pacing
Improvement is slower — reading speed and comprehension are harder to change quickly.

### Science (medium improvement potential)
Focus on: data interpretation strategy (not science knowledge — it's a reading comprehension test)
Most Science questions don't require science knowledge — they test whether you can read a graph or table quickly.

---

## What the Research Says About Retakes
- Average gain from retesting alone (no prep): +1–3 points
- With structured prep: +3–5 points
- After 3+ retakes with no improvement: likely at ceiling for current preparation level
- The biggest gains come between the 1st and 2nd attempt
- Score decreases on retakes are possible (rare, ~10% of students) but almost never by more than 1 point

## The TOPS Threshold Decision Tree
```
Current Score → Target → Gap → Plan
------------------------------------
Below 17      → 20     → 3+  → 10-week intensive plan, English + Math focus
17-19         → 20     → 1-3 → 6-week targeted plan, 2-3 sub-skill focus  
20-22         → 23     → 1-3 → 6-week targeted plan, weakest section focus
23-26         → 27     → 1-4 → 8-week plan, all sections, full test strategy
27-31         → 32     → 1-5 → 10-week plan, near-perfect strategy required
```
