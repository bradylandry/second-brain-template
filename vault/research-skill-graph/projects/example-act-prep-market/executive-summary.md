# Executive Summary — ACT Prep Market Research

## The One-Sentence Finding
Louisiana's mandatory ACT law + TOPS scholarship creates the highest-stakes, most captive test prep market in the US — and no existing app has built specifically for it.

## What We Learned

**The market is real and underserved.** ~50,000 Louisiana juniors take the ACT annually by law. The state average is ~18.2, meaning the majority score below the 20-point TOPS Opportunity threshold. The students scoring 17–22 are the exact cohort where prep produces the most reliable improvement (3–5 composite points with 20–30 hours of structured work). No competitor — at any price point — explicitly frames their product around TOPS thresholds.

**The value proposition is extraordinary.** Crossing from ACT 19 to ACT 20 unlocks ~$44,000 in TOPS scholarship money. A $49 app offering a credible path to that crossing has a 900x ROI for the user. This framing is unoccupied in the market.

**Free competition is real but beatable.** ACT Academy (official, free) and Khan Academy (free) exist but have high churn, no FSRS, no TOPS framing, and poor UX. The Khan Academy SAT precedent shows free tools don't kill paid market — they just raise the bar for what paid must offer.

**Louisiana alone is not enough.** ~14,000–20,000 realistically addressable students in Louisiana at 3% paid conversion yields modest revenue. National expansion to other high-mandate ACT states (Michigan, Wisconsin, Colorado, Alabama, Florida) is required for a sustainable business.

## What It Means for the Product
1. Lead with the TOPS gap visualization — first screen, every user
2. Add a score guarantee (3-point improvement or refund)
3. Price at $29–$49 one-time or $9.99/month
4. Build English and Math question banks deep before expanding to Science/Reading
5. Design for national expansion with state-configurable scholarship overlays from day one

## What We Still Don't Know
- Actual TOPS qualifying rate by income bracket (shapes pricing strategy)
- Current app retention/completion rates (Day-7, Day-30)
- School counselor ecosystem and B2B2C distribution path
- ACT format changes and question bank validity
