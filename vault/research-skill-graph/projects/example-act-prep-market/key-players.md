# Key Players — ACT Prep Market

## Direct Competitors

### ACT Academy (Free)
- **What it is:** Official free prep from ACT Inc., powered by Kaplan content
- **Strengths:** Official brand, free, covers all sections
- **Weaknesses:** No FSRS, no TOPS framing, poor UX (consistent complaint), high churn
- **Threat level:** Low — free but ineffective. More of a validation that users want something better.

### Khan Academy (Free)
- **What it is:** General ACT content; not an official ACT partner (unlike SAT)
- **Strengths:** Free, trusted brand, good explanations
- **Weaknesses:** No ACT-specific optimization, no adaptive personalization, no Louisiana/TOPS framing
- **Threat level:** Low for paid conversion, high for brand awareness

### Magoosh ACT ($130–$400)
- **What it is:** App + web prep with video explanations, 4-point score guarantee
- **Strengths:** 4.6/5 rating, score guarantee drives conversions, strong video explanations
- **Weaknesses:** No TOPS framing, no FSRS, no Louisiana focus, $130 floor is high
- **Threat level:** Medium — best direct comparable, but not competing on our positioning

### UWorld ACT (<$150)
- **What it is:** Question bank focused app, 3,200+ questions
- **Strengths:** Harder-than-real-test questions, excellent detailed explanations, strong dashboard
- **Weaknesses:** No AI tutor, no TOPS framing, not mobile-first
- **Threat level:** Medium — strong product, best question quality in market

### PrepScholar ($397)
- **What it is:** Adaptive web-based course with diagnostic
- **Strengths:** Strong diagnostic, adaptive study plan, free trial
- **Weaknesses:** Expensive ($397), web-only, no TOPS framing
- **Threat level:** Low — too expensive for Louisiana demographic

### Kaplan ACT ($180–$650)
- **What it is:** Live and on-demand courses; ACT Inc.'s official partner
- **Strengths:** Strong brand, live instruction option, official partnership
- **Weaknesses:** Expensive, not mobile-focused, no TOPS framing, conflict of interest (ACT Inc. partner)
- **Threat level:** Low for our price point

---

## Institutions & Structural Players

### ACT Inc.
- Controls the test, the official prep materials, and the Kaplan partnership
- Conflict of interest: profits from retakes AND prep sales
- Our positioning as independent/student-first is a direct counter to this

### Louisiana Board of Regents / LOSFA
- Sets TOPS thresholds, administers the scholarship
- Thresholds stable 20+ years — low risk of change
- Potential B2B2C partner: if they recommend the app, distribution is solved

### Louisiana Department of Education (LDOE)
- Mandates the ACT for all public school juniors
- Potential school counselor distribution channel
- Title I funding pathway (longer-term)

## Comparable Products to Learn From

### Duolingo
- Mastered freemium + gamification + spaced repetition at scale
- Streak mechanics drive daily retention
- Their model is the playbook for engagement, not outcomes

### Anki
- Proved FSRS/SRS has a passionate user base willing to pay
- Too complex for casual users — our app is the accessible version

### Photomath / Socratic
- Proved students will use mobile for academic help
- Instant answer + explanation format is what students expect
