## Research Question
What is the ACT prep market landscape — who are the real users, what do they actually need, who are the competitors, and how should we monetize the app?

## Scope
- **In:** Louisiana market (primary), US national market (context), students aged 15-18, TOPS scholarship context, mobile app monetization models
- **Out:** SAT prep, graduate test prep (LSAT/GMAT), international markets
- **Time Horizon:** Current market + 5-year outlook
- **Output Goal:** Inform product decisions — feature prioritization, pricing model, go-to-market strategy

## Quick Context

**The product:** ACT prep app for Louisiana students targeting the TOPS scholarship. Built on FSRS spaced repetition. Firebase auth, LLM tutor, Vercel deployment.

**The hypothesis:** Louisiana's mandatory ACT law + TOPS scholarship creates a captive, high-motivation market that national competitors aren't serving specifically. A Louisiana-focused app with TOPS score targets explicitly built in has a positioning advantage that Kaplan and Khan Academy can't easily replicate.

**Key tension to research:** Is the addressable paying market large enough to sustain the product, or does free competition (Khan Academy, ACT Academy) make monetization impossible?
