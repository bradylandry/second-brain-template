# Research Frameworks

How to approach different types of research questions. Read this BEFORE starting any research to pick the right structure.

## Framework Selection

### Type 1: "Is X true?" (Verification)
- Start with [[lenses/technical]] to establish what data actually says
- Then [[lenses/contrarian]] to stress-test the claim
- Then [[lenses/historical]] for precedent
- **Best for:** fact-checking, debunking, validating assumptions
- **Example:** "Is the ACT prep market actually growing?"

### Type 2: "Why is X happening?" (Causal Analysis)
- Start with [[lenses/historical]] to trace the roots
- Then [[lenses/economic]] to find incentive structures
- Then [[lenses/technical]] for mechanism
- Then [[lenses/contrarian]] to challenge your causal chain
- **Best for:** understanding trends, explaining phenomena
- **Example:** "Why are students struggling with ACT math specifically?"

### Type 3: "What happens if X?" (Scenario Planning)
- Start with [[lenses/first-principles]] to establish base assumptions
- Then [[lenses/technical]] for constraints
- Then [[lenses/economic]] for incentives
- **Best for:** forecasting, product strategy, decision-making
- **Example:** "What happens to ACT demand if colleges go test-optional permanently?"

### Type 4: "What should I do about X?" (Decision Support)
- Run all 6 lenses
- Rank options by lens agreement (if 5/6 lenses point the same way → high confidence)
- **Best for:** product decisions, pricing, feature prioritization
- **Example:** "Should the ACT app be free, freemium, or subscription?"

---

## Research Depth Levels

### Level 1: Quick Scan (30 min)
- 3 lenses maximum
- Top 5 sources only
- Goal: directional understanding, not certainty

### Level 2: Standard Research (2-3 hours)
- All 6 lenses
- 15-25 sources
- Goal: informed opinion backed by evidence

### Level 3: Deep Dive (1-2 days)
- All 6 lenses with sub-questions
- 50+ sources including primary data
- Goal: publishable analysis, decision-grade intelligence

---

## Source Collection Strategy

For each lens:
1. Start with the BEST single source (see [[source-evaluation]])
2. Find the source that DISAGREES most with #1
3. Find primary data that lets you judge between them
4. Record everything in [[../knowledge/data-points]] with attribution
